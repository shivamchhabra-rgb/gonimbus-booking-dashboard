5 · Block utilisation — Guaranteed revenue & load planning
Guaranteed revenue
Hard block & soft block bookings — monthly growth
Teleport's sales team sells blocked capacity to agents for a fixed period on a given O&D. Whether the agent utilises the space or not, this is committed revenue for Teleport — making block sales one of the highest-quality revenue streams in the business.
–
Total block bookings
Outcome
More block bookings through GC means more guaranteed, predictable revenue for Teleport — independent of actual cargo loaded. Beyond revenue, a higher concentration of block bookings gives the operations and load planning teams advance visibility of committed capacity, enabling better flight planning, ULD allocation, and ground handling coordination.
Hard block
–
Fixed capacity purchase. Teleport earns revenue regardless of whether the agent loads cargo.
Soft block
–
Reserved capacity with booking right, subject to confirmation. Strong demand signal for planning; commercial step toward hard block.
Hard block Soft block
–
Levers driving the metric
L-01Block surfaced first in route generation
GC's route ordering puts Hard Block and Soft Block at the top of every agent's route list. Agents naturally pick the first viable option — making block the path of least resistance and driving adoption without mandating it.
↑ Block revenue
L-02Sales team block configuration
Teleport's sales team can configure block profiles per route — defining capacity allocation, overbooking thresholds, and auto-confirmation percentages directly in GC. Accurate configuration converts agent demand into confirmed block sales efficiently.
↑ Guaranteed revenue
L-03Real-time capacity console for load planners
Load planners see block inventory status live and can open or close block space dynamically. This prevents premature closure (which leaves revenue on the table) and overcommitment (which creates operational risk).
↑ Load planning accuracy
L-04Automated FBL/CBA airline messaging
GC automatically triggers flight booking list (FBL) and capacity booking acknowledgement (CBA) messages to airline partners when block space is booked or modified — keeping capacity commitments in sync without manual intervention.
↑ Operational precision
✓  What went well
8.8× growth in block bookings (243 → 2,144) — entirely organic, driven by GC surfacing block inventory directly within the agent booking flow
Hard block and soft block growing at equal rates — agents are committing to hard block with confidence, not defaulting to the softer option
Acceleration in Apr–May 2026 aligned with GC crossing 50% agent share — more agents on GC = more block exposure = more guaranteed revenue
Block bookings provide load planning teams with committed forward capacity — reducing last-minute flight plan changes and improving ULD utilisation
→  How to improve
Block coverage by O&D is unknown — identify which high-volume routes have no block product sold, and prioritise the sales team to close those gaps
Soft block → hard block conversion rate should be tracked — agents on soft block are warm leads for the sales team to upgrade to committed hard block revenue
Agent training on block commitment — agents who understand hard block's commercial model (pay whether you load or not) make faster, more confident purchasing decisions
As migration completes, the growing GC agent base will have increasing access to block inventory — the sales team should have a proactive outreach plan ready to convert this demand
